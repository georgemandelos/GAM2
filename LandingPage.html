Mont Mégantic

Mont , located in Quebec, Canada, is known for its hiking trails and stunning views. The area around Mont Mégantic is part of Parc national du Mont-Mégantic, a provincial park. Here is a brief description of some hiking trails in the Mont Mégantic area: Le Mont Mégantic Summit Trail, Le Mont Saint-Joseph Trail, La Montée des étoiles Trail, La Montagne Noire Trail, and Le Tour de la Montagne Trail.

Parc Sutton

Mont Sutton, situated in Quebec's Eastern Townships, offers a diverse and well-marked network of hiking trails suitable for various skill levels. With options leading to the summit providing breathtaking panoramic views, hikers can immerse themselves in lush forests and explore the natural beauty of the region. The trails are accessible throughout the year, accommodating both summer hikers and winter enthusiasts interested in activities like snowshoeing.

Parc National de la Gaspésie

Parc National de la Gaspésie, located in the Gaspé Peninsula of Quebec, is a renowned destination for hiking enthusiasts. The park features an extensive network of trails that traverse diverse landscapes, including majestic mountains, dense forests, and pristine lakes. Hikers can explore a range of trails, each offering unique experiences, from challenging ascents to more leisurely walks. One of the highlights is the trail leading to Mont Albert's summit, providing panoramic views of the surrounding peaks. The park is home to diverse wildlife, and hikers may encounter caribou, moose, and various bird species along the way.

L'Acropole des Draveurs

L'Acropole des Draveurs, located in Parc National des Hautes-Gorges-de-la-Rivière-Malbaie in Quebec, offers a challenging and exhilarating hiking experience. Renowned for its stunning landscapes and rugged terrain, the trail to L'Acropole des Draveurs leads hikers through dense forests, rocky paths, and steep ascents. The highlight is reaching the summit, providing breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and the Malbaie River. The trail is known for its demanding nature, testing the endurance of hikers, but the rewarding vistas make it a memorable adventure.
